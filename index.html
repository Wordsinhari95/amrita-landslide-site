Reading the Land

A decade of landslide investigations across India

From the eastern Himalaya to the Western Ghats, the Amrita World Centre of Excellence on Landslide Risk Reduction (WCE‑LRR) has walked, measured and mapped landslide terrain across ten states. This atlas presents 94 documented sites — each with coordinates, classification and field observations — gathered through direct site investigation.

About this Atlas

Every site in this atlas was visited and assessed in the field by researchers from Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham's World Centre of Excellence on Landslide Risk Reduction (WCE‑LRR), an institution recognised under the UNESCO Chair on Experiential Learning for Sustainable Innovation and Development. Each entry follows a consistent data structure: a unique data code (Ls1–Ls93), GPS coordinates reconciled from field survey records, the landslide classification, and the date of the triggering event where known.

The sites span debris slides, debris flows, rockfalls, creep movements and catastrophic multi-wave landslides — including some of the most consequential landslide disasters in recent Indian history. Several locations also host permanent instrumentation: Deformation and Early-warning Platforms (DEPs) with geophones and rainfall gauges, contributing to long-term slope monitoring.

From Field Notes to Risk Reduction

Each site documented in this atlas contributes to a growing evidence base for landslide hazard assessment in India — informing slope stability models, early-warning thresholds, and land-use guidance for vulnerable hill communities. The WCE‑LRR continues to expand this record, returning to instrumented sites for long-term monitoring and adding new investigations after major events.
